Double Pendulum Simulation

As a Computer Science student, I've always been fascinated by chaos theory and how simple systems can produce complex behaviors. The double pendulum is a perfect example of this phenomenon, which led me to create this interactive simulation.

How It Works

A double pendulum consists of two weights (called "bobs") connected by rigid rods. The second pendulum is attached to the end of the first one. While a single pendulum moves in a predictable way, adding a second pendulum creates a chaotic system.

The motion is influenced by:

  • Gravity pulling both weights down
  • The momentum of each pendulum affecting the other
  • The connecting point between the pendulums transferring forces

Interesting Behaviors

  • Small changes in starting position can lead to completely different patterns
  • At high energy, the motion becomes chaotic and unpredictable
  • At low energy, it can show more regular, periodic motion

Technical Details

The simulation uses physics principles like conservation of energy and momentum. To keep the system stable and interesting for longer periods, it:

  • Maintains a target energy level to prevent the pendulum from eventually stopping
  • Applies subtle energy corrections to compensate for numerical approximations
  • Uses a small damping factor to prevent unrealistic energy gains
  • Limits maximum speeds to avoid numerical errors
  • Updates positions 60 times per second for smooth animation
